Poetry

icarus

Since when did the earth revolve around the sun?
It always has and always will.
Even in the winter solstice,
when the earth is farthest from the sun,
I will always gravitate back to you.

Honey, you are the sun,
and if by the arrogance of Icarus I fall,
then fall I will.

untitled

a steep mountain hike

and i am a taboo,

rosequartz

Oh, sweet Sappho, I beg of you, hear me:
She is a rose—gentle and kind and fair;
Her smile gives me butterflies I can't bear,
a bliss like jumping off a cliff to sea.
How can someone so soft like her be?
Sappho, help me; my lungs are out of air.
I love her, so I whisper a quick prayer
That I be worthy to be loved by she.

fishbone
I already know the answer to my own question and yet the truth stays lodged in my throat like a fishbone: you aren't coming back. Swallowing the truth stings my throat, and it bleeds. You aren't coming back, it repeats, insistent on digging in deep. There's no other choice but to devour it whole, lest I let it consume me.
